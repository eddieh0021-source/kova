Members are the people who use your Kova Hub CAD — dispatchers, officers, firefighters, and anyone else in your ER:LC community. You control who joins, which department they belong to, and what they can do. Every member authenticates with Discord, so there are no separate passwords to manage on your end.

## Inviting members

Share your server's unique invite link to bring community members on board. Anyone who follows the link and logs in with Discord is added to your server automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your invite link">
    Open **Manage Server** and navigate to the **Members** tab. Your server's
    invite link is displayed at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share the link">
    Post the invite link in your community's Discord server — a dedicated
    onboarding channel works well so new members can find it easily.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Member logs in with Discord">
    When a member follows the link, they are prompted to log in with Discord
    OAuth. Kova Hub uses their Discord identity to create their account; no
    additional sign-up form is required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Member appears in your roster">
    Once logged in, the new member appears in your **Members** list with a
    default unassigned status. Assign them to a department to grant CAD access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Pin the invite link in a dedicated `#cad-access` channel and remind members
  that they must use the same Discord account they use for your community
  server.
</Tip>

## Assigning departments

A member without a department assignment cannot access any CAD panel. Assigning them to a department — and giving them a role within it — is what unlocks their access.

1. In **Manage Server**, open the **Members** tab.
2. Click the member's name to open their profile.
3. Under **Department**, choose from your available departments (for example, Police, Fire/EMS, DOT, or any custom department you have created).
4. Select an appropriate **Role** within that department to set their permission level.
5. Save the changes — the member can now access the relevant CAD panel on their next login.

You can assign a member to more than one department if their role in your community spans multiple services. Each department assignment carries its own independent role and permission set.

<Note>
  Not sure which departments to create? See [Server Setup](/management/server-setup)
  for guidance on configuring departments when you first build your server.
</Note>

## Managing active members

During a live CAD session you can see which members are currently on duty directly from the session view. Active session data lets you:

- See which units are signed on across each department in real time.
- Monitor call assignments and unit availability without leaving the CAD panel.
- Review session logs after a session ends to audit activity or resolve disputes.

Session data is stored against your server and is retained as long as the server exists, giving you a running history of community activity.

## Removing members

If someone leaves your community or should no longer have CAD access, remove them from your server's member list.

<Warning>
  Removing a member **immediately revokes their CAD access**. They will be
  signed out of any active session and will not be able to rejoin until they
  are re-invited. Make sure you intend to cut access before confirming the
  removal.
</Warning>

To remove a member:

1. Go to **Manage Server → Members**.
2. Click the member's name to open their profile.
3. Select **Remove Member** and confirm the action.

The member's session history remains in your logs for record-keeping, but they lose all active permissions and department assignments immediately.
