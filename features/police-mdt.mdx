The Police MDT (Mobile Data Terminal) gives every officer in your department a dedicated workspace for records management. Use it to run civilian and plate lookups, issue warrants, broadcast BOLOs, write arrest reports, and log citations — and attach any of those records directly to an active dispatch call so your whole team stays in the picture.

<Note>
  Every record type in the MDT — warrants, BOLOs, arrests, and citations — can be attached directly to an active dispatch call. Open the record, click **Attach to Call**, and select the call from the list. The record becomes visible to all units assigned to that call.
</Note>

## Civilian & Plate Lookup

Before issuing any record, start with a lookup to confirm the subject's identity and pull their history.

- **Name search** — Enter a first name, last name, or both to find a civilian profile. Results show existing records, active warrants, and prior arrests.
- **Plate search** — Enter a full or partial plate number to retrieve the registered owner, vehicle description, and any BOLOs associated with that plate.

Lookups return results instantly from your community's record database. No result found means the subject has no records on file — not that they do not exist in the system.

## Record Types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Warrants">
    A warrant flags a civilian as wanted and alerts any officer who runs their name.

    **To create a warrant:**

    1. Run a name lookup and open the civilian's profile.
    2. Click **New Warrant**.
    3. Enter the charge, penal code (for example, `211 PC — Armed Robbery`), and any relevant notes.
    4. Click **Issue Warrant** to make it active.

    The warrant appears on the subject's profile immediately. Any officer who later runs that name will see the active warrant highlighted at the top of the results. You can also attach the warrant to an open dispatch call so responding units are aware before they make contact.

    To clear a warrant after an arrest, open it and click **Mark Served**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="BOLOs (Be On the Lookout)">
    A BOLO broadcasts a vehicle or person description to all active officers so they can watch for the subject in the field.

    **To issue a BOLO:**

    1. From the MDT home screen, click **New BOLO**.
    2. Choose whether the BOLO is for a **vehicle** or a **person**.
    3. Fill in the description — for a vehicle, include make, model, color, and plate (for example, *Black Sentinel, plate ABC123*). For a person, include physical description and last known clothing.
    4. Enter the **last seen location** and any relevant notes.
    5. Click **Broadcast BOLO**.

    The BOLO pushes to all connected MDT sessions. Officers can acknowledge it directly from their MDT. Once the subject is located and the situation is resolved, mark the BOLO **Resolved** to remove it from the active feed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Arrest Reports">
    An arrest report documents a custodial arrest and ties it permanently to the subject's record.

    **To write an arrest report:**

    1. Run a name lookup and open the subject's profile, or start from an active dispatch call.
    2. Click **New Arrest Report**.
    3. Enter the arresting officer(s), charges with penal codes (for example, `23152 VC — DUI`), narrative, and any evidence notes.
    4. Click **Submit Report** to save it to the subject's record.

    Submitted arrest reports cannot be deleted, only amended with a supplemental note — maintaining an accurate, tamper-evident history. Attach the report to the originating dispatch call to link the arrest to the incident timeline.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Citations">
    A citation logs a traffic stop or code violation without making a custodial arrest.

    **To issue a citation:**

    1. Run a name or plate lookup to identify the subject.
    2. Click **New Citation**.
    3. Select the violation type — **traffic** (for example, speeding, failure to stop) or **code** violation.
    4. Enter the specific charge, fine amount if applicable, and any officer notes.
    5. Click **Issue Citation** to save it to the subject's record.

    Citations appear in the civilian's record history alongside arrests and warrants, giving you a complete picture of their prior contact with your department.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
